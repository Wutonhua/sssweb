FINANCIAL AID

This is where you will find useful information and application for financial aid.

FAFSA - Free Application for Federal Student Aid

The Free Application for Federal Student Aid (FAFSA) is a crucial tool for students in the United States seeking financial assistance to pursue higher education. Managed by the U.S. Department of Education , FAFSA enables students to apply for various forms of federal financial aid, including grants, loans, and work-study programs. By gathering information about a student's or their family's financial situation, such as income and assets, FAFSA calculates the Expected Family Contribution (EFC), which determines the student's eligibility for aid.

The FAFSA is available annually starting on October 1st for the upcoming academic year. Students are encouraged to fill out the FAFSA as soon as possible after it becomes available, as some aid programs have limited funds distributed on a first-come, first-served basis.

All types of students who are considering pursuing higher education should fill out the FAFSA. This includes:

  1. 1. High school seniors planning to attend college or university after graduation.
  2. 2. Current college students who are continuing their education.
  3. 3. Graduate students pursuing advanced degrees.
  4. 4. Non-traditional students returning to school after a break.
  5. 5. Students attending vocational or technical schools.
  6. 6. Students pursuing certificates or other non-degree programs.

CSS - College Scholarship Service Profile

The CSS Profile, unlike the FAFSA, is an additional financial aid application required by some colleges and universities in the United States, particularly private institutions. Here are the types of students who may need to fill out the CSS Profile:

  1. 1. Students applying to colleges or universities that require the CSS Profile.
  2. 2. Students seeking additional financial aid opportunities
  3. 3. International students
  4. 4. Students with complex financial situations
  5. 5. Students applying for specific scholarships or aid programs

It's essential for students to check the financial aid requirements of each college or university they are applying to determine whether they need to submit the CSS Profile in addition to the FAFSA. Additionally, students should be aware of deadlines, as it may differ from institution to institution.

SCHOLARSHIP

Applying to scholarships is a strategic investment in oneself that not only alleviates financial burdens but also recognizes achievements, provides opportunities, and fosters personal and academic growth. Below are some common categories of scholarships one can apply:

  1. 1. Academic Scholarships: Awarded based on academic performance
  2. 2. Merit-Based Scholarships: Granted to students who demonstrate exceptional abilities, talents, or accomplishments in areas like athletics, performing arts, leadership, or community service
  3. 3. Need-Based Scholarships: Designed for students with demonstrated financial need
  4. 4. Minority Scholarships: Targeted towards specific demographic groups, including racial or ethnic minorities, LGBTQ+ individuals, women in STEM fields, and other underrepresented populations.
  5. 5. Field of Study Scholarships: Available for students pursuing degrees in particular fields or disciplines, such as STEM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics), humanities, business, healthcare, or vocational programs.
  6. 6. Military and Veteran Scholarships: Available to active-duty military personnel, veterans, and their dependents, often offered by military branches, veterans' organizations, or government agencies.
  7. 7. Essay Contests and Creative Scholarships: Require applicants to submit essays, creative works, or projects on specific topics or themes
